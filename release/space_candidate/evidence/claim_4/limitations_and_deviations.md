# Claim 4 limitations and deviations

This is a proof-backed verification of a universal theoretical proposition,
not a claim that finitely many empirical models establish universality. The
full-scale references are exact evaluations at the paper's sample counts; no
13-dimensional MALA chain is presented as evidence for the theorem.

The paper's informal “any candidate model” wording is narrower than all
possible probability laws: its proof assumes a continuous radial CDF and
nested metric-ball regions. Atomic candidates or distance ties require a
randomized probability-integral transform and are outside this audited
contract unless that extension is supplied.

The appendix says equality occurs iff supports are disjoint, while the main
text says equality is approached for disjoint supports. This campaign verifies
the lower inequality. It does not promote the stronger equality
characterization as a separately verified claim.
